Indian Rocks Beach, Florida

Your Gulf
Coast Guide

Sun, sand, and everything in between.

A barrier island gem on Pinellas County's Gulf Coast — known for calm turquoise water, spectacular sunsets, and an unhurried local rhythm.

Plan Your Visit
Day-Of Essentials

Beach Conditions
& Essentials

Water & Surf

  • Water Temp: 72°F (winter) to 86°F (summer)
  • Gulf Conditions: Generally calm — ideal for swimming & paddleboarding
  • Surf: Minimal, flat to ankle-high most days
  • UV Index: High year-round — SPF 30+ recommended

Parking

  • Gulf Blvd: Metered street parking available
  • Free Lot: Beach Access #2 — limited spots
  • Pro tip: Arrive before 9am on peak weekends (May–Sep)
  • Overflow: Nearby plazas on weekdays

Amenities

  • Restrooms: 1st St, 19th Ave, 27th Ave accesses
  • Showers: Outdoor rinse stations at main accesses
  • Lifeguards: Not on duty — swim at your own risk
  • Accessibility: Wheelchair beach mats available (contact Pinellas County Parks)

Best Times to Visit

Shoulder season (Apr–May, Sep–Oct) offers warm water, lower crowds, and better rates. The Gulf faces west — sunrise doesn't hit the water, but sunset is spectacular. Golden hour starts about 45 minutes before sunset; plan accordingly.

Know Before You Go

Beach Rules
& Local Tips

Beach Rules

  • ✕ No alcohol on the beach (city ordinance)
  • ✕ No fires or open flames
  • ✕ No glass containers
  • ✕ No driving on the beach
  • ✕ Fireworks prohibited
  • 🐕 Dogs on leash only — before 9am & after 5pm at designated access points
  • 🐢 Sea turtle nesting season: May 1–Oct 31 — do not disturb nests; no white lights facing beach after dark

Local Tips

  • ◆ Bring a beach cart — parking can be a 5–10 min walk from the sand
  • ◆ Water shoes recommended — occasional shell beds near shore
  • ◆ Arrive early on holiday weekends — Gulf Blvd gets gridlocked by 11am
  • ◆ Check Pinellas County's marine forecast before renting a kayak or paddleboard
  • ◆ Bring cash — some local vendors and parking meters are card-inconsistent
  • ◆ Pack a trash bag — keep the beach beautiful; bins fill fast on busy days
  • ◆ Stay hydrated — Gulf Coast heat index climbs fast in summer; aim for 8oz water per hour in direct sun

Outdoor water park with slides and a lazy river, right next to the beach. Great for families looking for a break from the waves.

Outdoors

Pinellas Trail

Paved multi-use trail connecting the county. The IRB connector links directly to the countywide network — ideal for cyclists and joggers.

Accessible from Bay St

Kayaking · Water

Hurricane Pass

A popular launch point for kayaks and paddleboards into the Intracoastal. Calm water, mangrove shorelines, and frequent dolphin sightings.

West Bay Dr at the pass

Shopping · Dining

Johns Pass Village
& Boardwalk

Waterfront boardwalk village with shops, restaurants, fishing charters, and boat tours. A classic Florida experience just 3 miles south.

Madeira Beach, ~3 miles south

Wildlife · Education

Suncoast Seabird
Sanctuary

One of the largest wild bird hospitals in the US — free to visit. Pelicans, herons, and recovering raptors up close. Genuinely moving.

18328 Gulf Blvd, IRB
